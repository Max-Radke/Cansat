ğŸš€ What is a CanSat?

A CanSat is a small satellite the size of a soda can, which is launched by a rocket to an altitude of about 700 meters, where it performs measurements during its descent. These miniature satellites allow students to experience real space technology and conduct scientific experiments.

The name "CanSat" is derived from "Can" (can) and "Sat" (satellite), perfectly describing the size and shape of our project.

ğŸ”¬ Scientific Background

Our CanSat will contain various sensors to measure important atmospheric parameters:

  • Temperature: Measurement of air temperature at different altitudes
  • Air Pressure: Determination of air pressure during the flight
  • Humidity: Measurement of air humidity
  • GPS: Positioning and flight path tracking

These data will help us better understand the atmosphere and provide valuable insights for science.

The development of a CanSat presents us with several technical challenges:

  • Development of robust electronics that can withstand the rocket launch
  • Programming of sensors and data transmission
  • Design of a stable casing
  • Implementation of a parachute system
  • Creation of a ground station for data reception
