🎯 Our Mission

Learn more about our primary and secondary missions in the CanSat Competition 2025/26.

📡 Primary Mission: Air Pressure and Temperature Measurement

The primary mission is the same for all teams. It involves measuring air pressure and temperature during the flight and transmitting the data via a radio connection to a ground station (e.g., laptop). From the obtained data, the teams are required to develop an altitude and temperature profile and determine the descent rate.

  • Air Pressure Measurement: Continuous recording of air pressure during the entire flight
  • Temperature Measurement: Precise measurement of air temperature at different altitudes
  • Radio Transmission: Real-time transmission of measurement data to the ground station
  • Altitude Profile: Development of a detailed altitude profile from the air pressure data
  • Temperature Profile: Creation of a temperature profile of the atmosphere
  • Descent Rate: Determination of the CanSat's descent rate

🔬 Secondary Mission: Venera-Inspired Atmospheric Probe

Our secondary mission is inspired by the Venera probes that traveled to Venus. We plan to design an innovative "probe" that performs various advanced measurements and technologies during the flight.

  • Livestream: Real-time video transmission during the flight for visual documentation
  • Gas Measurements: Measurement of CO, CH₄, H₂, NO₂, NH₃ in the atmosphere using available sensors
  • Altitude Determination: Determination of altitude using the air pressure formula
  • Ground Distance: Measurement of distance from the ground using RADAR or LIDAR at around 100m distance
  • Stabilization: Optional system for stabilizing the satellite during the flight

🎯 Mission Goals

Our primary goal is to develop a fully functional CanSat that successfully completes both missions. We strive for excellence in the technical execution and scientific evaluation of our data.

📊 Expected Results

Through our missions, we expect the following scientific and technical insights:

🔬 Scientific Insights

  • Detailed temperature profiles of the atmosphere
  • Air pressure changes at different altitudes
  • Correlation between altitude and atmospheric conditions
  • Validation of weather models

⚙️ Technical Goals

  • Integration of available camera systems for CanSats
  • Optimization of data transmission with standard components
  • Use of commercially available sensors
  • Efficient evaluation of collected flight data
